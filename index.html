Radiocarbon Probabilities

New methodologies leverage radiocarbon data through advanced probability modeling.

These techniques can illuminate the migration, growth, and expansion patterns of ancient populations by gathering large volumes of radiocarbon sample data and analyzing the frequency of individual radiocarbon dates.

Symbology

Many sites contain more than one artifact. Symbols are semi-transparent: brighter circles indicate more higher-probability samples in one area.

The controls on the right side change symbol color according to the sample's associated archaeological culture. Not all samples are associated with a culture.

Archaeological Cultures
Bell Beaker pottery vessel. Credit: Natural History Museum of London
Archaeological cultures describe common elements found in the material remains of ancient population groups. While cultures can be distinctive, these labels can also obscure underlying processes of change within groups, as well as important differences between them.

What Was Left:

Archaeology and the Radiocarbon Record of the UK
A Story in Bone
In this map, you're invited to explore the radiocarbon history of the UK.

Each dot represents a unique artifact which has been radiocarbon dated. Most sites contain several artifacts, often from different eras and cultures.

Photo Credit: Alan Hawkes, The Sandman
Time Slider and Symbols
The map covers a time sequence from 10,000 BCE to 1820 CE.

As you move the time slider, Symbols will change size according to a probability density function: the larger the symbol, the more likely it originated in that year.

Archaeological Culture
Buttons change symbol color according to the sample's associated culture.
Symbols are semi-transparent. Brighter colors suggest that several artifacts may overlap in time at that site.
Symbols are encircled by white rings. More rings at one site suggest several artifacts which don't overlap in time.
